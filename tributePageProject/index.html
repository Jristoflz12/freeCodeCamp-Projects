Chester Bennington

The voice of a generation

1976-2017

Chester Bennington was an American singer and songwriter who was best known as the lead vocalist of rock band Linkin Park. He was also the lead vocalist of the bands Grey Daze, Dead by Sunrise, and Stone Temple Pilots.

Here are some of his highlights

  • Chester Charles Bennington was born on March 20, 1976, in Phoenix, Arizona.
  • Bennington first began singing with a band called Sean Dowdell and His Friends? in the early 90's. Later, he joined another band called Grey Daze.
  • In 1999 he joined Mike Shinoda, Rob Bourdon, Brad Delson and Joe Hahn and their band Xero, which was then renamed Hybrid Theory
  • Bennington and the band, now called Linkin Park released their first album in late 2000. It was a massive success worldwide; so much so that it became the best selling-album of 2001.
  • In 2005, Bennington co-founded Dead by Sunrise, an electronic rock band from Los Angeles, California, with Orgy and Julien-K members Amir Derakh and Ryan Shuck.
  • In 2006, he married Talinda Ann Bentley, with whom he had three children: Tyler Lee Bennington and twins Lily and Lila.
  • Bennigton joined Stone Temple Pilots in 2013, band which he dreamed of becoming a member of during his youth. He left the band on good terms due to his commitments with Linkin Park in 2015.
